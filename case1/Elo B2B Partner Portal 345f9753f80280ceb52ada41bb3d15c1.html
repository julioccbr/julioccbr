1. Overview

Redesigning the Elo B2B Partner Portal.

Context: Elo’s B2B Portal is the central hub for major corporate partners. Recognizing growing operational friction, I proactively investigated user and technical pain points. I pitched the redesign initiative to leadership, taking full ownership of the project to evolve the portal from a legacy system into a scalable, modern platform.

Goal: Standardize visual components on a new Design System bridging UX and Engineering, and restructure the Information Architecture (IA), all executed autonomously under a strict 30-day deadline.

2. Problem

Two conflicting Design Systems and a broken architecture.

As the solo designer on the project, I noticed growing symptoms of structural and visual inconsistencies impacting both partner autonomy and internal operations. To validate these suspicions, I proactively orchestrated alignment sessions with Engineering, Product Managers, and Support teams. This investigation surfaced four critical blockers, which formed the basis of my pitch to leadership for a complete redesign:

3. Solution

AI-assisted workflow, fixing UI and architecture in 30 days.

Operating under a strict 30-day MVP deadline, I bypassed traditional, time-consuming design bottlenecks. I engineered a lean, AI-driven workflow focused on high-impact cross-functional alignment and rapid execution.

Phase 1: Validation & Strategic Prioritization

I facilitated a Value vs. Effort Matrix session with Engineering and PMs to map out the technical debt’s impact. We evaluated each feature based on technical complexity (new components, refactoring) against strategic value for the partners. This allowed me to ensure engineering efforts were focused entirely on high-impact workflows, strategically deferring secondary features.

Based on the matrix, I negotiated a phased rollout organized by the color-coded sprints shown above. We prioritized foundational architecture (Auth0, IA) and core access flows, culminating in the critical AC Elo feature. Meanwhile, complex legacy services were explicitly de-prioritized to the future roadmap to protect the MVP's technical feasibility within our strict deadline.

Phase 2: Visual Translation with Google Stitch & Metaprompting

The new "Eloquent" Design System did not yet cover the specific complex components required for the portal. To prevent a design-to-development bottleneck, I implemented an AI-assisted UI generation workflow using Google Stitch:

The AI acted as a visual translator, rapidly generating modern UI concepts and replacing intrusive interactions with clean side-sheets. This eliminated the ideation bottleneck and allowed me to focus exclusively on refining the high-fidelity UI.

Phase 3: AI-Driven Information Architecture (IA)

With the UI foundation set, the portal’s hierarchy required a complete overhaul to reduce cognitive load. Lacking the time for weeks of manual card-sorting, I engineered a custom Gemini AI Agent. I fed the model the complete list of features and quantitative usage metrics. The AI processed this data to suggest grouping recommendations based on established B2B mental models (billing, management, products), allowing me to rapidly define a scalable and intuitive navigation tree.

4. Results & Handoff

Pixel-perfect prototypes and a standardized Design System.

The redesigned product secured 100% cross-functional approval, effectively solving the visual and technical debt while establishing a clear path for future feature rollouts, both for this project and any other that utilizes the Eloquent design system.

Visual Excellence

I delivered pixel-perfect, high-fidelity prototypes that successfully aligned the complex B2B workflows with Elo's new, modern visual identity, completely resolving the legacy visual debt.

Design System & Developer Handoff

Beyond the interface, this project served as a catalyst for the "Eloquent" Design System’s growth. I focused on creating a frictionless handoff process that empowers developers to implement components with 100% fidelity.
